Programs Offered

Pitching to investors is a “right-of-passage” for entrepreneurs. Raising funds usually takes much more time than founders expect. It often takes dozens – even a hundred or more meetings to meet the investment goals.

Many people think pitching to investors is about following a slide template and learning how to talk in front of the room.

But it’s much more than that.

Make an effective pitching includes the ability to:

  • Create a vision with personal credibility.
  • Understand and speak to the investor mindset.
  • Present and discuss your plan in a brief, powerful way like a seasoned executive.
  • Use key metrics built upon customer validation, research and go-to-market planning.
  • Acknowledge and manage risk; (many founders do everything to discount risk which does not work).
  • Demonstrate being “coachable, adaptable and responsible.”
Benefits

A person trained to make effective presentations will dramatically increase their effectiveness. The results include:

  • A huge improvement in confidence and presentation skills,
  • Less time hunting funds,
  • Potentially higher valuation and better terms, and,
  • Better relations and expectations with investors.
Objectives

When you take the full program the result is a complete pitch deck and the ability to present an effective story with and without slides.

  • Make an effective pitch deck and make a compelling presentation to investors.
  • Use body language and clear speaking to establish credibility and trust.
  • Realize the importance of credibility and being realistic while building a vision for the future.
  • Understand and speak to the investors point of view.
  • Tell the story so that investors will clearly understand your value proposition and potential so they may make an investment decision.
  • Present your company in an elevator pitch, (30-60 seconds), and in 2 to 4 minutes with and without using slides.
  • Show confidence in verbal presentation. Cover the key business metrics. Make clear, simple explanations.
  • Understand the investors point of view.
  • Have a completed pitch deck for presentation on projectors. Add brief content to a second version for mailing out.
  • Optional: have a 1-page executive overview completed.
Duration
  • 2 hours or half day, and
  • 1 to 10-day programs, depending on your needs and budget.

Normally 4 days are needed to get to a basic proficiency level for college grads with a few years work experience and a working prototype. Each day after that allows us to increase skills with the attendees.

Partial day workshops focus on skills and making effective slides.

Multiday workshops focus on building the foundation up to presentation and useful forecasts and go-to-market plans.

Note: We know from experience that a newer team with a working product needs at least four days to get a solid plan and presentation put together. We will provide a pre-workshop either via a video, or live presentation 1 to 2 weeks before the event to help the startups to prepare.
